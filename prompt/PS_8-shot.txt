Question: Mitchell is trying to chew as many pieces of gum at once as he can. He has 8 packets of gum, There are 7 pieces in each. If he chews all the gum except for 2 pieces, how many pieces does he chew at once?
Plan:
Step 1: Calculate the total number of gum pieces Mitchell has.
Step 2: Subtract the number of gum pieces Mitchell does not chew.
Solution:
Step 1: Total number of gum pieces = 8 packets * 7 pieces = 56 pieces
Step 2: Number of pieces chewed = Total pieces - pieces not chewed = 56 pieces - 2 pieces = 54 pieces
Therefore, Mitchell chews 54 pieces of gum at once.

Question: Fred has 38 red marbles, half as many green ones, and the rest are dark blue. If he has 63 marbles, how many of them are dark blue?
Plan:
Step 1: Calculate the number of green marbles Fred has.
Step 2: Subtract the number of red and green marbles from the total number of marbles to find the number of dark blue marbles.
Solution:
Step 1: Number of green marbles = 38 red marbles / 2 = 19 green marbles
Step 2: Number of dark blue marbles = Total number of marbles - red marbles - green marbles = 63 marbles - 38 marbles - 19 marbles = 6 marbles
Therefore, Fred has 6 dark blue marbles.

Question: There are 92 students altogether. Twenty of them ride the school bus home together, 5/8 of the remaining ride their own bike home, and the rest whose houses are near the school walk home. How many students are walking home?
Plan:
Step 1: Calculate the number of students who don't ride the school bus.
Step 2: Calculate the number of students who ride their own bikes home.
Step 3: Subtract the number of students who ride the bus and bike from the total number of students to find the number of students who walk home.
Solution:
Step 1: Number of students not riding the bus = Total students - Students who ride the bus = 92 students - 20 students = 72 students.
Step 2: Number of students who ride their bikes = 5/8 * 72 students = 45 students.
Step 3: Number of students who walk = Total students not riding the bus - Students who ride bikes = 72 students - 45 students = 27 students.
Therefore, 27 students are walking home.

Question: Brianne saves $10 in January. Each month, she saves twice as much as her previous month's savings. How much will she save in May?
Plan:
Step 1: Calculate how much Brianne saves in February.
Step 2: Calculate how much Brianne saves in March.
Step 3: Calculate how much Brianne saves in April.
Step 4: Calculate how much Brianne saves in May.
Solution:
Step 1: February savings = January savings * 2 = $10 * 2 = $20.
Step 2: March savings = February savings * 2 = $20 * 2 = $40.
Step 3: April savings = March savings * 2 = $40 * 2 = $80.
Step 4: May savings = April savings * 2 = $80 * 2 = $160.
Therefore, Brianne will save $160 in May.

Question: A school bus has 4 columns and 10 rows of seats. If the school has 6 buses, how many students can the buses accommodate?
Plan:
Step 1: Calculate the total number of seats in one bus.
Step 2: Calculate the total number of seats in all the buses.
Solution:
Step 1: Total seats in one bus = Number of columns * Number of rows = 4 columns * 10 rows = 40 seats.
Step 2: Total seats in all buses = Number of buses * Seats in one bus = 6 buses * 40 seats = 240 seats.
Therefore, the buses can accommodate 240 students.
